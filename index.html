INTRODUCTION TO LANDSLIDES

Landslides occur when gravitational and other types of shear stresses within a slope exceed the shear strength
(resistance to shearing) of the material that form the slope. Also, it is the downward and outward movement of Soil, rock or vegetation under the influence of gravity

CAUSES OF LANDSLIDES

  • Earthquakes: Earthquakes occurs when the earth’s crust suddenly breaks and releases energy which
    leads to loose of rocks and soil which can trigger landslides
  • Heavy rainfall: Heavy rainfall can saturate the ground causing debris flow,
    which is a type of landslide that involves flowing materials.
  • Human activities: construction, deforestation, and mining can all contribute to landslides.

Types of land

  • Rock fall : These happens when rocks break off a cliff face and fall down the hillside.
  • Debris Flow: Is a type of landslide that occurs when a mixture of water and sediment,
    such as mud, rocks and vegetation moves rapidly down a slope often causing damage and loss of life.
    It can also be known as mudslides or debris floods which can occur in burn areas.
  • Slumps: It’s a type of landslide that occurs when a mass of rock or soil slides down a slope,
    often in a rotational motion. It is characterized by a distinct scrap(head scrap)at the top of the slope
    and a toe( foot) at the bottom of the slope.

FACTORS CONTRIBUTING TO LANDSLIDES

  • GEOLOGICAL FACTORS Rock type and structure: Weak or fractured rock, such as shale or sandstone are more prone to landslides. Slope angle and orientation: Steep slopes, especially those facing downhill are more susceptible to landslides.
  • CLIMATIC FACTORS. Drought and wildfires: Prolonged drought can weaken vegetation wile wildfires can alter soil properties increasing the rate of landslides. Temperature Fluctuations: Extreme temperatures fluctuations can cause soil and rock to expand and contract leading to instability.
  • HUMAN ACTIVITIES. Deforestation and land use change: Removing vegetation can increase soil erosion and instability. Mining and guarrying: Extracting minerals or rock can weaken soil leading to landslides
